---
title: Skellige
category: regions
---

Skellige is an archipelago of fierce, seafaring clans whose cuisine is dictated by the sea and a harsh northern climate. Fish—herring, cod, mackerel—is the backbone of the diet, almost always preserved by salting, smoking, or pickling to survive winter storms that cut off supply. Mutton and goat substitute for beef given the rocky terrain's poor grazing. Mead, brewed from local honey, is the signature drink, served in great quantities at clan feasts alongside roasted whole animals. Skellige cooking is blunt, hearty, and short on delicate seasoning; dried seaweed, juniper, and coarse salt provide most of the flavor. Bread is dense, made from barley and rye, and shared communally at long tables in clan longhouses.
