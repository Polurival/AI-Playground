---
title: Toussaint
category: regions
---

Toussaint is a sun-drenched southern duchy famed for its vineyards, chivalric culture, and refined cuisine. Its warm climate supports abundant fruit, herbs, and vegetables unavailable further north, and its cooking is the most delicate in the setting: reduced wine sauces, butter-based preparations, and fruit pairings with duck, lamb, and poultry. Toussaint wine—both red and white—is considered the finest in the known world and features constantly in its recipes, not just as a drink but as a cooking ingredient. Truffle-scented mushrooms, olives, and fresh herbs appear far more often here than in northern kingdoms. Where Temerian or Redanian food is about warmth and sustenance, Toussaint cuisine is about pleasure, presentation, and pairing—closer to fine dining than survival fare.
