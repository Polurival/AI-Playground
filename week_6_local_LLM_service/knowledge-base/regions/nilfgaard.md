---
title: Nilfgaard
category: regions
---

Nilfgaard is the vast southern empire whose cuisine reflects both military efficiency and imperial ambition. Army provisioning favors dense, portable foods: hardtack, dried meat, and meat pies that travel well on long campaigns. Nilfgaardian meat pie, filled with seasoned beef or pork and root vegetables in a sturdy pastry crust, is emblematic of this practical style. Away from military rations, the imperial court enjoys richer fare influenced by conquered southern territories, including spiced meats and stronger use of pepper and exotic imported seasoning than northern kingdoms typically have access to. Wine is more common than ale in Nilfgaardian culture, and formal banquets emphasize disciplined, structured courses that mirror the empire's rigid administrative character.
