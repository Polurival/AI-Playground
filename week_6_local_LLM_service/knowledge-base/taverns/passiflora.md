---
title: The Passiflora
category: taverns
---

The Passiflora is a luxurious establishment in Novigrad, better known as a high-end house of pleasure than a tavern, but its kitchen holds its own against any restaurant in the city. It serves refined small plates meant to accompany an evening of entertainment: candied nuts, spiced wine, delicate pastries, and rich pâtés served with sweet preserves. The kitchen imports Toussaint wine and fruit to match the establishment's upscale clientele, and dishes are plated with more care than function demands—presentation matters as much as flavor here. Though not a place travelers stop for a hearty meal, its influence on Novigrad's upper-class dining trends is notable, and its recipes for spiced wine and sweet pastries have been copied by lesser establishments across the city.
