---
title: The Kingfisher Inn
category: taverns
---

The Kingfisher Inn is a modest establishment in a Skellige fishing village, built from driftwood and stone against the coastal wind. Its menu centers on the day's catch—herring, cod, and mackerel—prepared simply: pickled, smoked over alder wood, or pan-fried with butter and dill. The inn is also known for its hearty mutton stew, served on cold nights to fishermen returning from the sea, and for pouring generous portions of mead brewed by the innkeeper's own family. There is no pretense of refinement here; portions are large, prices fair, and the fire in the hearth rarely goes out. Travelers passing through the isles often mention the Kingfisher's smoked herring as some of the best in Skellige.
