---
title: Ard Skellig
category: locations
---

Ard Skellig, the largest island of the Skellige archipelago, is a windswept land of fjords, longhouses, and fishing villages. The sea dominates the diet: herring, cod, and mackerel are salted, smoked, or pickled to survive the harsh winters, and shellfish is gathered from the rocky shoreline. Livestock is hardy and sparse, so mutton and goat appear more often than beef. Skellige cooking is blunt and hearty—thick fish stews, dense rye and barley breads, and generous use of dried seaweed and juniper for flavor when fresh herbs are scarce. Great halls host feasts after successful raids or hunts, roasting whole animals over open fires and serving mead in enormous drinking horns. The island's isolation means imported spices are rare and prized; most flavor comes from smoke, salt, and the sea itself.
