---
title: Beauclair
category: locations
---

Beauclair, capital of Toussaint, is a sunlit city of vineyards and chivalric splendor. Its cuisine is the most refined in the Northern Realms, drawing on abundant local produce: grapes, plums, and berries from the surrounding hills, truffle-scented mushrooms, and duck raised on nearby estates. Toussaint chefs favor delicate sauces built from reduced wine, butter, and fruit preserves, a style quite different from the heavier fare found further north. Duchess Anna Henrietta's court is famous for multi-course banquets pairing each dish with a specific regional wine. Even common taverns in Beauclair serve dishes with more finesse than northern capitals—duck breast with plum sauce, herb-crusted lamb, and delicate fruit tarts are everyday fare rather than special occasion food. The city's markets overflow with fresh herbs, olives, and cheese unmatched elsewhere in the setting.
