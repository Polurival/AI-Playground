---
title: Forest Mushrooms
category: ingredients
---

Forest mushrooms, foraged from the woodlands of Temeria and Kaedwen, are a cornerstone of northern cooking, adding deep umami flavor to stews, sauces, and roasted dishes. Varieties are dried and strung up in kitchens to preserve them through winter, then rehydrated in broth or wine when needed. Dried mushroom broth forms the base of many meatless soups eaten during religious fasting days observed by followers of the Eternal Fire. Fresh mushrooms, gathered in autumn, are often sautéed with butter and garlic as a simple side or folded into pierogi filling. Foragers must know their varieties well, as several toxic look-alikes grow alongside edible species in the same forests—local knowledge passed down through generations is essential to safe mushroom cookery.
