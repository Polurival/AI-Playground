---
title: Smoked Eel
category: ingredients
---

Smoked eel is a prized delicacy along the rivers and coasts of the Northern Kingdoms, particularly around Novigrad's Pontar delta and the fishing villages of Skellige. Eel is caught in traps set along muddy riverbanks, then smoked slowly over alder or oak wood, giving it a rich, oily flavor that keeps for weeks without refrigeration. It is typically served sliced thin as a starter with pickled vegetables and rye bread, or flaked into soups and pierogi filling. Novigrad market stalls sell smoked eel as a portable snack for dockworkers, while wealthier households serve it as a refined appetizer before larger meals. Its intense, smoky flavor makes it a favorite pairing with strong ale or dry white wine.
