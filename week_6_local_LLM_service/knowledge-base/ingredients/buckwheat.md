---
title: Buckwheat
category: ingredients
---

Buckwheat is a hardy grain grown widely in the northern kingdoms, valued because it thrives in poorer soil and cooler climates where wheat struggles. Cooked into a coarse groats or kasha, it serves as a filling side dish for stews and roasted meats, especially in Redania and Kaedwen. It also gets ground into flour for dense, dark bread and pancakes. Because buckwheat stores well and cooks quickly, it is a common provision for travelers, soldiers, and witchers on the road. In tavern kitchens, buckwheat groats are often fried with onion and bacon fat to make a simple, filling dish that stretches a small amount of meat across a larger meal—a practical staple of northern peasant cooking.
