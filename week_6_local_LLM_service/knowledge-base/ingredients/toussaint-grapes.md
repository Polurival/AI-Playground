---
title: Toussaint Grapes
category: ingredients
---

Toussaint's vineyards produce the finest grapes in the known world, the foundation of the duchy's celebrated wine industry. Beyond wine, fresh grapes appear in Toussaint cooking as a pairing for duck and poultry dishes, added whole or reduced into a sauce with butter and stock. Dried into raisins, they flavor sweet pastries and spiced breads found throughout southern markets. Toussaint grape varieties are cultivated on terraced hillsides under near-constant sun, giving them a sweetness northern fruit rarely matches. Even grape leaves are used, wrapped around seasoned rice or meat and gently braised, a technique borrowed from trade contact with lands further south. Toussaint chefs consider the grape as essential to their identity as venison is to Temerian cooking.
