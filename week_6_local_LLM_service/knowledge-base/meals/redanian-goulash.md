---
title: Redanian Goulash
category: meals
---

Redanian goulash reflects southern trade influence on a northern kingdom, combining local pork with paprika-forward spicing uncommon further north.

Ingredients: 600g pork shoulder (cubed), 1 onion (chopped), 1 tbsp paprika, 1 clove garlic (minced), 1 cup stock, 1 potato (diced), 1 tbsp lard, salt, caraway seed.

Method: Melt the lard in a heavy pot and cook the onion until golden. Remove from heat briefly and stir in the paprika so it doesn't scorch, then return to heat and add the pork, browning it on all sides. Add garlic, caraway, and stock, cover, and simmer gently for about an hour until the meat is tender. Add the diced potato in the last 20 minutes so it cooks through without falling apart. Season with salt to taste. Serve with buckwheat groats or dark bread to soak up the paprika-rich sauce, and a mug of herbal vodka on the side is traditional in colder months.
