---
title: Nilfgaardian Meat Pie
category: meals
---

Nilfgaardian meat pie is a dense, portable dish born from military provisioning, designed to survive long marches without spoiling.

Ingredients: 500g beef or pork (diced), 1 onion (chopped), 2 carrots (diced), 1 cup stock, 1 tbsp flour, black pepper, bay leaf, shortcrust pastry (double batch for base and lid).

Method: Brown the meat in a hot pan, then set aside. Sauté onion and carrot in the same pan until softened, sprinkle in flour, and stir to coat. Return the meat, add stock and bay leaf, and simmer covered until the meat is tender and the liquid has thickened into a gravy, about 45 minutes. Season generously with black pepper. Let the filling cool, then spoon into a pastry-lined pie tin, top with a pastry lid, seal the edges, and bake until golden brown, roughly 30–35 minutes at a moderate oven heat. Served cold or reheated, it keeps well for days, making it a favorite among traveling soldiers and mercenaries.
