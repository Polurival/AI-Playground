---
title: Kaedweni Bigos
category: meals
---

Bigos is a thick, long-simmered cabbage and meat stew found throughout the northern kingdoms, with the Kaedweni version known for using whatever smoked meats are on hand, making it a favorite at Kaer Morhen and other remote keeps.

Ingredients: 500g mixed meat (smoked sausage, pork, or leftover roast, cubed), 1 small cabbage (shredded), 1 cup sauerkraut, 1 onion (chopped), 2 dried mushrooms (soaked and sliced), 1 tbsp lard or oil, bay leaf, black pepper, splash of stock or water.

Method: Render the lard in a large pot and brown the meat pieces. Add the onion and cook until soft, then stir in the cabbage, sauerkraut, and mushrooms. Pour in enough stock to keep the pot from drying, add bay leaf and pepper, and simmer uncovered on low heat for at least two hours, stirring occasionally. Bigos improves with reheating over successive days, developing deeper flavor each time, which suits its origin as a stew meant to stretch scarce meat across a long winter. Serve with dark rye bread.
