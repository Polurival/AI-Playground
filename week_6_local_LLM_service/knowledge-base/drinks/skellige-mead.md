---
title: Skellige Mead
category: drinks
---

Mead, fermented from honey and water, is the signature drink of the Skellige Isles, brewed in nearly every clan household and served in great quantities at feasts. Island beekeeping supplies the honey, and recipes vary by clan, some adding berries or herbs to sweeten or sharpen the flavor. Mead is central to Skellige social life—clan gatherings, victory celebrations, and funerals alike involve passing drinking horns brimming with mead around the long table. It ranges from light and sweet to strong and heady depending on fermentation time, with older batches reserved for special occasions such as a jarl's feast. Unlike Toussaint wine, prized for elegance, Skellige mead is valued for its role in communal ritual—drunk together, in large amounts, as an expression of clan bonds.
